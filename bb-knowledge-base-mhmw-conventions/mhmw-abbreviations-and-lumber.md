# MHMW Abbreviations, Terms, and Lumber Guide

MHMW's in-house glossary of the abbreviations and terms that appear on structural
and shop drawings, plus the shop's lumber-sizing reference. This is the "read the
callout correctly" layer for any agent interpreting an FC/submittal drawing set.

Source: `MHMW 101.pdf` (pp. 1–2) / `Abbreviations and Lumber.pdf`.

### Terms with fuller definitions

- **Ledger** — material (normally angle iron) fastened to the building, made to
  hold brick.
- **Lintel** — material (normally angle iron) *not* fastened to the building
  (normally bears on brick on both sides) to hold brick above an opening.
- **Shear wall and holdowns** — a wall constructed to withstand lateral forces and
  prevent uplift; connected to the concrete slab using holdowns.

## Lumber Guide

### Dimensional Lumber

Standard types: douglas ("doug") fir, spruce, hem fir. **Actual sizes differ from
nominal dimensions:**

| Nominal dimension | Reduction | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ≤ 1" | reduced by ¼" | — |
| > 1" and < 7" | reduced by ½" | 2×4 = 1½" × 3½" |
| > 7" | reduced by ¾" | 6×12 = 5½" × 11¼" |

### Engineered Lumber

Standard types: Glulam, LVL. **Actual dimensions do NOT differ from nominal.**

Example: a `1¾" × 11¼" LVL` is actually 1¾" × 11¼".

> **Why this matters for review:** when reconciling a wood member callout against a
> steel connection (ledger, lintel, headers bearing on lumber), use the *actual*
> dimension for dimensional lumber and the *nominal* dimension for engineered lumber.
